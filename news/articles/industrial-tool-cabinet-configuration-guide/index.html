Industrial Tool Cabinet Configuration Guide

Drawers, lock strategy, and side-mounted PDU standards for OEM/ODM projects.

Published: Feb 20, 2026 | Updated: Feb 20, 2026

Direct Answer

For most factory programs, the most practical tool cabinet setup is a mixed-depth drawer layout, a lock type chosen by access policy, and an upper side-mounted PDU selected by destination market socket standard. Configure by real workflow data first, then choose options. This avoids over-spec builds, reduces retrieval time, and improves first-sample approval.

TL;DR

  • Plan drawer zones by tool family and frequency, not by drawer count alone.
  • Use recessed mechanical lock for low-maintenance reliability, smart lock for traceability.
  • Set side-mounted PDU position and plug standard at RFQ stage to avoid redesign.

Lock And PDU Option Rules

  • Use recessed mechanical lock for low-maintenance and long service cycles.
  • Use smart lock where identity-based control and usage traceability are needed.
  • Install integrated power module on side upper panel in horizontal orientation.
  • Offer socket standards by market package: US, UK, EU, and configurable variants.
  • Define service access and replacement method in the first engineering drawing set.

FAQ

What drawer count is practical for most projects?

There is no single perfect count. Start by mapping tools to usage frequency and physical size, then assign mixed drawer depths. This produces better workflow than selecting by total drawer quantity alone.

Can we offer both mechanical and smart lock options?

Yes. This is common in export programs. Keep the cabinet platform stable and make lock type a configurable option so different markets can choose the control level they need.

Where should PDU modules be mounted?

For serviceability and ergonomics, upper side placement in horizontal orientation is widely practical. Confirm socket standards and cable routing in the engineering BOM.
